About

Description:

My name is Brian Singer and I am currently a junior at Byram Hills High School. For the past couple of years I have taught myself computer programming and photography. I love working on projects that involve microcontrollers and/or computers. I also enjoy running track and playing ultimate frisbee on the weekends. Recently, I have been focussing on my science research project where I am attempting to detect bruxism (excessive tooth grinding and/or clenching) with a minimal number of electrodes. I am the co-founder of JGrader.com, an online grading system for Java. I am always excited to learn new technologies and programming languages.

Projects

jGrader

Description:

jGrader is a cloud based grading system designed for use within a high school AP Computer Science program. The system allows teachers to easily collect, grade, and provide feedback on assignments. Teachers can execute code submitted by their students directly in their browsers. Over 100 teachers and 600 students use the website.

How I built this website:

jGrader.com uses a node.js express stack, the database is mySQL and the frontend uses the EJS templating engine with standard javascript (jquery) and CSS. The private git repository is on www.github.com. I have spent over 200 hours on this project.
